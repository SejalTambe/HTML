Novel Summaries

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

Set in the 1930s, this novel follows Scout Finch, a young girl growing up in the racially divided town of Maycomb, Alabama. As her father, Atticus Finch, defends a black man accused of raping a white woman, Scout learns about empathy, morality, and the unjust nature of prejudice and discrimination.

1984 by George Orwell

In a dystopian future, the totalitarian regime of Oceania is ruled by the oppressive Party led by Big Brother. Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the Party, rebels against its control and falls in love with Julia, leading to their doomed struggle against the surveillance state and the eradication of individuality.

The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

Narrated by Nick Carraway, the novel explores the decadence and moral decay of the Jazz Age. Jay Gatsby, a wealthy and enigmatic man, is obsessed with the idea of reclaiming the love of his life
